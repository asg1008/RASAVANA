    Why Native Plants?

    Plant native wildflowers in your area. The big benefit is that they are well suited to this environment, meaning they will flourish almost fully without any maintenance. This helps the local ecology while saving you money and time.

    • Native plants are more resilient and require less water, fertilizer, pesticides, and care than exotic plants due to their adaptation to local conditions and pests.
    • Growing native plants supports local biodiversity by providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
    • Flowers of native plants offer nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
    • Seeds and fruits from native plants supply nourishment for birds and small mammals.
    • Planting native species helps prevent the spread of invasive plants that could disrupt local ecosystems and displace native species.
    • Consider lavender, rosemary, lemon thyme, lemongrass, and mint to repel insects.
    • Prefer low-maintenance options like succulents and herbs.

    Minimise Water Waste

    Choose efficient irrigation methods such as drip irrigation or, for small-scale gardening, a water can. Save rainwater in a bucket at the corner of your garden or rooftop to water your plants.

    • Use drip irrigation for direct water delivery to roots, minimizing waste and evaporation. For small gardens, a watering can allows for targeted watering for efficient irrigation.
    • Collect rainwater in buckets or barrels from your rooftop or garden to provide a sustainable irrigation source.
    • Rainwater is free from impurities and minerals found in tap water, promoting healthier plant growth.
    • Utilizing rainwater and efficient irrigation reduces reliance on municipal water supplies and lowers environmental impact.
    • Collecting rainwater and using efficient systems can lower your water bills while supporting eco-friendly gardening.

    Soil Care

    Proper soil practices are essential for eco-friendly gardening, as they significantly improve soil quality and promote microflora biodiversity. Adding compost and organic matter enriches the soil, enhancing its fertility and natural properties. Additionally, reducing tillage practices allows beneficial soil organisms to flourish and supports effective carbon storage. By adopting these practices, gardeners can create a healthier, more sustainable environment for their plants.

    Avoid the application of chemals Applying synthetic pesticides and fertilisers can harm the environment and Wildlife →
    Instead, choose natural pest control and fertilisation methods such as:

    • Avoid Chemicals: Steer clear of synthetic pesticides and fertilizers to protect the environment and wildlife.
    • Mechanical Weed Removal: Use manual or mechanical methods to remove weeds effectively.
    • Natural Pest Control: Utilize biological pest control methods, such as traps and beneficial predators like mites and ladybugs. Consult local agricultural shops for tailored advice.
    • Homemade Repellents: Create natural pest repellents using ingredients like garlic, chili sprays, and neem oil.
    • Organic Fertilization: Enhance soil fertility by applying compost and manure for organic fertilization.

    Promote Biodiversity

    Your garden and balcony can create a vibrant ecosystem, a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical surroundings.

    Biodiversityrefers to the variety of life forms within an ecosystem. In essence, the healthier the conditions are for your ecosystem, the greater the biodiversity you will experience in your garden or on your balcony.

    • Create Habitats: Establish areas in your garden that support birds, insects, and other wildlife, such as hedges or nesting spots.
    • Provide Water Sources: Ensure there are accessible water sources, especially during the summer months.
    • Mow Less Frequently: Reduce mowing to allow lawn weeds like dandelions to flower, which serve as valuable food sources for pollinators.
    • Grow Pollinator-Friendly Plants: Select plants that are beneficial for pollinators to enhance biodiversity.
    • Ensure Year-Round Blooming: Grow a variety of plants that flower throughout the year to provide consistent food sources.
    • Choose Simple Flowers: Avoid plants with double or multi-petaled flowers, as they can hinder insect access to pollen.

    Minimise Waste and Compost

    • Reuse Plant Pots: Make use of your plant pots by reusing them whenever possible. If you no longer need them, consider recycling. Many nurseries accept plastic pots for reuse.
    • Choose Sustainable Materials: Opt for sustainable options like wood when building your plant beds.
    • Follow Disposal Instructions: If you use chemical fertilizers and pesticides, be sure to follow label instructions for proper cleaning and disposal.
    • Make Homemade Compost: Don’t hastily discard your garden’s organic waste. Instead, create homemade compost to produce nutrient-rich humus that enhances soil productivity.
    • You can also add kitchen leftovers to your compost, thus minimising kitchen waste. The main ingredients you can add to your compost are:

      Grass/plant fragments
      Dry leaves
      Chopped wood/bark
      Fruit and vegetable peels
      Coffee grounds
      Eggshells
      Shredded newspaper
